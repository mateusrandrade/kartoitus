History, Memory & Technology

I am a historian interested in what happens when the past meets technology.

Looking at history today means navigating fragments. The attempt to transform messy archival materials into structured knowledge inevitably confronts us with the limits of computation. Yet, this is not a purely technical challenge: the way we organize knowledge also shapes what becomes visible, plausible, or even imaginable.

This space emerges from that friction. The goal here is not to prove the world, but to think through it carefully—attentively and in motion.

— Mateus Andrade
