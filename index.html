History, Memory & Technology

I am a historian interested in what happens when the past meets technology.

Historical work has always meant navigating fragments. Archives rarely present themselves as complete narratives; they demand interpretation, inference, and careful attention to traces, absences, and unstable relations. Today, the attempt to transform archival materials into structured knowledge confronts us not only with technical challenges, but also with the limits of computation itself. Yet, this is not a purely technical challenge: the way we organize knowledge also shapes what becomes visible, plausible, or even imaginable.

This space emerges from that friction. The goal here is not to prove the world, but to think through it carefully—attentively and in motion.

— Mateus Andrade
